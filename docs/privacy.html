Privacy Policy

Last updated: June 25, 2026

Rclone Next ("the App") is developed by m3rcha. This policy describes what data the App handles on your Mac. The App is open source: github.com/m3rcha/rclone-next.

Summary

Rclone Next does not run its own servers and does not sell your data. The App runs locally on your Mac and shells out to your installed rclone binary. Your files and cloud credentials are managed by rclone under your existing configuration.

Data stored on your device

  • App preferences — e.g. custom rclone path, welcome screen state, saved mount list (via macOS UserDefaults on your Mac).
  • rclone configuration — stored by rclone in your user account (typically ~/.config/rclone/rclone.conf). Rclone Next reads this through the rclone CLI; it does not upload your config to us.

Network activity

  • Update checks — the App may contact the GitHub API to compare its version against published releases, and runs rclone version --check for rclone update info.
  • rclone operations — any transfer, browse, mount, or sync connects to cloud providers you configured in rclone. That traffic goes between your Mac, rclone, and those providers — not through Rclone Next servers.
  • Documentation links — opening help links (e.g. rclone.org) uses your browser or system default handler.

What we do not collect

  • No analytics or advertising SDKs
  • No account system or cloud backup of your usage
  • No collection of file names, remote contents, or passwords by the App developer

Third parties

Cloud storage providers (Google Drive, OneDrive, S3, etc.) are configured and accessed through rclone. Their privacy policies apply to data stored with them. See rclone's privacy information.

Children

The App is not directed at children under 13.

Changes

We may update this policy on this page. Continued use of the App after changes constitutes acceptance.

Contact

Questions or concerns: open a GitHub issue.
